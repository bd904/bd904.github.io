Biological Research

Population Genomics • Epigenetics • Metagenomics • Molecular Biology

Research Focus

Dr. Dubay's work spans the genomics of both model and non-model plant species, with a strong emphasis on how organisms adapt to challenging environments. Her core expertise lies in population genomics, molecular epigenetics, metagenomics, and host-microbe interactions. Her research provides insights into the evolutionary mechanisms shaping plant genomes and traits under environmental stress.

Highlighted Projects

  • Prosopis Multi-omics Research (UAEU) – Decoding the multi-omics landscape of Prosopis species to investigate adaptation strategies in arid climates.
  • EpiDiverse (EU Horizon-2020) – Genome assembly/annotation and Mapping population-scale variation and other elements in Populus nigra for trait association.
  • Plant Virus Host Range Evolution (CBGP-UPM, Spain) – Studied the spread of RNA viruses in wild and cultivated plants using metagenomic tools.
  • Plant-Nematode Symbiosis (IARI, India) – Genome assembly and annotation of Photorhabdus bacteria and other plant nematodes for sustainable pest management strategies.

Methodologies

Her methodological toolkit includes NGS data analysis, genome assembly, annotation pipelines, methylation analysis, comparative genomics, and custom scripting in Bash, R, and Perl. She routinely integrates computational pipelines with biological insight to interpret complex genomic datasets.
